FAQs

Chelsea FC was founded in 1905 at The Rising Sun pub (now The Butcher’s Hook) in Fulham, London. The club's home ground, Stamford Bridge, has been its base since the beginning. Chelsea joined the Football League shortly after its formation and quickly gained a reputation for attracting large crowds. While the club had early flashes of promise, major success eluded them until 1955, when they won their first First Division title. The 1960s and 70s brought more silverware, including the FA Cup (1970) and UEFA Cup Winners’ Cup (1971).

The 1980s were turbulent, with financial issues and on-pitch struggles, including relegation. However, the 1990s marked a revival, thanks to high-profile players and the arrival of managers like Ruud Gullit and Gianluca Vialli, leading to FA Cup and League Cup wins. A turning point came in 2003, when Russian billionaire Roman Abramovich bought the club. His investment transformed Chelsea into a global football powerhouse.
